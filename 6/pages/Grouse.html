Grouse /\xc9\xa1ra\xca\x8as/ are a group of birds from the order Galliformes, in the family Phasianidae. Grouse are frequently assigned to the subfamily Tetraoninae (sometimes Tetraonidae), a classification supported by mitochondrial DNA sequence studies,[1] and applied by the American Ornithologists\' Union,[2] ITIS,[3] and others.[4] Grouse inhabit temperate and subarctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere, from pine forests to moorland and mountainside,[5] from 83\xc2\xb0N (rock ptarmigan in northern Greenland) to 28\xc2\xb0N (Attwater\'s prairie chicken in Texas).[6]
